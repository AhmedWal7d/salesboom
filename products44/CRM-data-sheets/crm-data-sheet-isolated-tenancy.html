Understanding Isolated Tenancy: Your Private CRM Environment

Isolated Tenancy represents a hybrid deployment model that combines the best elements of dedicated hosting and multi-tenant cloud solutions. Instead of sharing server resources with other organizations, your CRM instance runs on dedicated infrastructure maintained exclusively for your business.

The Fundamental Principle

Salesboom deploys the complete CRM platform on a separate server dedicated to a single tenant—your organization. This approach delivers the economic benefits and continuous innovation of cloud-based software while providing the security, performance, and control traditionally associated with on-premises or dedicated hosting solutions.

Ideal Deployment Scenario

This deployment option is ideal for organizations seeking the advantages of cloud CRM without compromising on data isolation, performance guarantees, or regulatory compliance requirements. You receive all the standard Salesboom services, tools, and features available in regular deployments, but with the added assurance of maintaining one data source and system exclusively for your use.

Isolated Tenancy vs. Multi-Tenant vs. On-Premises

Understanding the differences between deployment models helps organizations make informed decisions aligned with their specific requirements.

Multi-Tenant Deployment

Best For:

Small to medium businesses, organizations prioritizing cost efficiency, standard CRM requirements

Advantages:

Lowest cost, fastest deployment, automatic updates, minimal IT overhead, proven scalability

Considerations:

Shared infrastructure, standard customization limits, performance can vary during peak usage

Isolated Tenancy Deployment

Best For:

Mid-market to enterprise organizations, regulated industries, performance-critical operations, complex customization needs

Advantages:

Dedicated infrastructure, guaranteed performance, enhanced security, deep customization flexibility, customizable SLAs, significantly lower cost than on-premises

Considerations:

Higher cost than multi-tenant, still managed by vendor, requires clear business justification

On-Premises Deployment

Best For:

Extremely regulated environments, organizations with specific data residency requirements, complete control requirements

Advantages:

Maximum control, data remains on-premises, complete customization freedom

Considerations:

Highest total cost of ownership, significant IT resources required, hardware refresh cycles, disaster recovery complexity, slower access to innovation

Key Decision Factors

Budget and total cost of ownership • Regulatory and compliance requirements • Performance and scalability needs • IT resource availability and expertise • Customization and integration complexity • Security and data privacy requirements • Business criticality of CRM operations
