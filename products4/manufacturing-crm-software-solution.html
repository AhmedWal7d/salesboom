Why Manufacturing Requires Specialized CRM Solutions

The Manufacturing Industry accounts for approximately one quarter of world economic activity. From Lean Manufacturing to Just In Time (JIT) processes—each manufacturing approach has unique requirements that demand specialized CRM capabilities.

Critical Manufacturing Challenges

  • Manual processes that slow production and increase errors
  • Disconnected systems creating information silos across departments
  • Lack of visibility across the supply chain and partner networks
  • Difficulty tracking specifications, quality control, and regulatory compliance
  • Inefficient quote-to-order processes resulting in delays and lost revenue
  • Complex product configurations and pricing structures
  • Difficulty managing longer-term customer relationships spanning years or decades

Manufacturing Process Requirements

Cloud CRM solutions must recognize that each manufacturing process requires sharing joint databases both in-house and with external partners, absolute inventory control, and tight scheduling capacity to deliver smooth and reliable production processes.

Whether using Lean Manufacturing, Flexible Manufacturing, Agile Manufacturing, Rapid Manufacturing, or JIT processes, manufacturers need systems that adapt to their specific operational workflows while maintaining integration across all business functions.

Services That Address Critical Manufacturing Challenges

Salesboom offers Cloud CRM services specifically designed to address priority business needs in the Manufacturing Industry. Our platform tackles the most pressing operational challenges manufacturers face daily.

Territory Management

Automate assignments based on geography, organizational division, product specialty, and customer characteristics. The system intelligently identifies and assigns the appropriate team member or members to each customer account, ensuring the right expertise is matched with the right client every time.

This eliminates territory conflicts and ensures optimal resource allocation across your manufacturing representatives, distributors, and partners.

Relationship Management

Understand complex customer relationships including subsidiaries, partners, distributors, and industry associations. Retain and access comprehensive relationship information that prospects or customers have with other organizations to construct rewarding partnerships in sales and ongoing customer relationships.

This is particularly critical in manufacturing where business relationships span years or decades, requiring deep institutional knowledge and relationship continuity.

Product Tracking and Catalog Management

Catalog sizes and specifications for products in an online reference library accessible across your organization. Create multi-tiered product hierarchies that reflect your actual product structures. The system accommodates real-time updates to product structure and pricing to reflect production changes, ensuring your teams always work with current information.

  • Track lot, batch, and serial numbers with complete traceability
  • Monitor component-level details and product registration
  • Document site of manufacture and production history
  • Maintain complete service history for warranty and compliance

Quote Management and Pricing Control

Indicate threshold levels for discounting by percentage and manage pricing breaks and volume discounts with precision. Manage multiple pricing options to ensure quotes are accurate, complete, competitive, and valid.

The system offers tools that facilitate quicker transfer of quotes into orders, dramatically reducing sales cycle time. Workflow automation routes product engineering and pricing requests automatically to the right decision-makers, eliminating bottlenecks.

Process Automation and Workflow

Codify business processes to eliminate manual steps and reduce errors. Support the addition of new applications and databases as your needs evolve. Digitize catalog and content management functions for assembling and publishing catalogs from data sources distributed across your organization.

The result: Reduced change orders, fewer clarification calls, and dramatically reduced expedited ordering through fewer production changes. Your team works smarter with automated workflows handling routine tasks.
