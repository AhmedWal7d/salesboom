RFI, RFQ, and RFP: Understanding the Procurement Process

Each procurement document serves a distinct purpose in vendor evaluation and selection.

Request for Information (RFI)

An RFI is a preliminary document used to obtain information from potential CRM providers before a formal solicitation document is issued.

Key Purposes:

  • Identify qualified CRM vendors in the marketplace
  • Verify service levels align with best practices
  • Confirm if using a particular CRM is justified
  • Solicit external solutions to business challenges
  • Gather technical specifications and capabilities
  • Understand pricing models and approaches

Salesboom welcomes RFI submissions and provides comprehensive responses detailing our capabilities, experience, and approach.

Request for Quotation (RFQ)

An RFQ is a solicitation document used to obtain pricing information from qualified CRM vendors. Unlike an RFP, the RFQ focuses primarily on cost.

Characteristics:

  • Requested for information and pricing purposes
  • Vendors submit quotes for specific requirements
  • Quotes are not automatically binding offers
  • Requires confirmation before final agreement
  • Focuses on pricing and commercial terms
  • May include SLA and support pricing
  • Often follows an RFI after pre-qualification

Our pricing model offers predictable monthly costs starting at $14 per user with volume discounts available.

Request for Proposal (RFP)

An RFP is a comprehensive document that invites vendors to propose complete CRM solutions including software, services, and implementation.

RFP Should Include:

  • Detailed functional and technical requirements
  • Current system environment and integration needs
  • Timeline and budget constraints
  • Evaluation criteria and scoring methodology
  • Implementation expectations and metrics
  • Support and training requirements
  • Security and compliance requirements

Our RFP responses address every requirement with specific details, implementation timelines, and clear pricing.
