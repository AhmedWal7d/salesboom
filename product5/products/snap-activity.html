Why Most Teams Struggle with Activity Management

Sales, marketing, and service teams juggle multiple tools—separate task managers, disconnected calendars, email reminders, sticky notes, and spreadsheets. This fragmentation creates chaos:

The consequences are predictable and costly:

  • Accountability disappears when tasks aren't tied to customer records

The Result?

Strategy doesn't translate into execution. Plans remain just plans. Revenue suffers because work that should happen doesn't happen—or happens too late.

Best Practices for Maximum Impact

Technology only delivers value when it's used effectively. The most successful Salesboom users follow these proven best practices to maximize the impact of activity management features.

Daily Habits

Start Every Day from the Home Tab: Make the CRM your first stop each morning, not your email inbox. Review your agenda, check business metrics, and plan your day before diving into reactive tasks. This simple habit shift transforms productivity.

Always Set a Next Task on Opportunities: Never leave an opportunity without scheduling the next action. Even if it's just "follow up in two weeks," capturing that commitment ensures the deal keeps moving and nothing falls through the cracks.

Use Events for Real Commitments, Not Reminders: Reserve calendar events for scheduled interactions—calls, meetings, demos, site visits. Use tasks for action items. This distinction keeps your calendar focused on time-based commitments while maintaining a separate list of to-dos.

Review Your Agenda Twice Daily: Check your agenda at the start of each day to plan and at the end to prepare for tomorrow. This bookending habit ensures you're always one step ahead and never caught off guard.

Customization Strategies

Personalize Shortcuts by Role: Sales reps should have shortcuts to pipeline and forecast reports. Marketers need campaign dashboards. Service teams require case queues. Executives want high-level business intelligence. Take five minutes to customize shortcuts for each team member.

Surface Relevant Metrics: Different roles care about different metrics. Configure the Home screen to show signups for marketing, pipeline velocity for sales, ticket resolution time for service. Make the dashboard personally relevant.

Create Standard Task Templates: Build reusable task lists for common processes—onboarding new customers, closing deals, resolving escalations. Templates ensure consistency and reduce the mental load of figuring out what to do next.
