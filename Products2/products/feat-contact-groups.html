The Limitation of One-Size-Fits-All CRM Systems

Most CRM providers force businesses into a single organizational model—either contact-centric or account-centric. This rigid approach creates frustration when your business needs don't fit neatly into one category.

The Challenges Businesses Face

  • Sales teams need to organize contacts by geography, industry, or role regardless of which company they work for
  • Marketing teams want to segment contacts by interests, engagement level, or campaign participation
  • Service teams require grouping by support tier, product ownership, or service agreement type
  • Growing businesses need flexibility as their organizational needs evolve over time
  • Multi-divisional companies require different organizational structures for different departments

Traditional CRM systems force you to choose one model and stick with it, creating inefficiencies and workarounds that waste time and reduce user adoption. Your CRM should adapt to how you work, not the other way around.
