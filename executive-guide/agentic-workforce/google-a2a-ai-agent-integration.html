Why Enterprise AI Agents Still Can't Work Together

Enterprise AI has reached a critical inflection point. Organizations have invested heavily in copilots, assistants, and domain-specific AI agents, yet most of these systems operate in complete isolation.

The Current Reality Creates Operational Chaos

  • Marketing uses one AI platform with its own data and capabilities
  • Sales deploys different AI tools that cannot access marketing insights
  • Finance, HR, and IT each maintain separate agent systems
  • Customer service operates independently from all other departments
  • Cross-functional workflows still require humans to manually bridge gaps between systems

These agents are individually powerful but fundamentally incapable of teamwork. The result is vendor lock-in, operational fragmentation, and persistent human bottlenecks in workflows that span departments. Your AI investments deliver isolated improvements rather than enterprise-wide transformation. Google A2A (Agent2Agent) was introduced specifically to solve this fundamental interoperability problem. It represents the infrastructure layer that transforms isolated AI tools into coordinated agent networks.

The Strategic Shift: Siloed AI Era to Connected Agent Networks

The current state of enterprise AI represents what we call the "Siloed AI Era." In this era, AI agents are embedded inside specific platforms, each possessing deep expertise but operating with narrow scope. Cross-functional workflows still require humans to manually bridge gaps between disconnected systems.

Agent Networks with A2A

Google A2A enables the transition to true agent networks with fundamentally different characteristics:

  • One agent acts as the Lead Agent (Orchestrator) coordinating the overall workflow
  • Specialized agents act as Remote Agents, each contributing their unique expertise
  • Tasks are delegated horizontally across systems without human intervention
  • Results flow back automatically with verification and context intact

Real-World Example: At-Risk Deal Recovery

Consider a complex sales scenario that previously required multiple meetings and email threads:

  1. A sales agent detects a deal at risk based on customer engagement patterns
  2. The agent automatically opens a task with a finance agent to evaluate discount impact on margins
  3. Simultaneously, a legal agent checks contract terms and compliance requirements
  4. A service agent confirms delivery feasibility and current capacity
  5. All findings are aggregated and presented to the sales team with recommendations

This entire sequence can happen autonomously via A2A protocols—no meetings, no email threads, no delays. The sales team receives a comprehensive analysis in minutes instead of days.

How Google A2A Works: Agent Cards, Tasks, and Artifacts

Executives don't need to understand the complete protocol specification to grasp A2A's transformative value. The architecture simplifies into three core concepts that work together seamlessly.

1

Agent Cards: The AI Resume

A standardized, machine-readable description of what an agent can do. Functions as the agent's resume published to the network, including skills, permissions, costs, security requirements, and supported formats.

2

Tasks: The Work Request

Structured work requests passed between agents with clear objectives, context, and success criteria. Tasks include priority levels, deadlines, required inputs, and expected outputs.

3

Artifacts: The Deliverable

The verified results returned by agents—reports, analyses, processed data, or generated documents. Artifacts are versioned, auditable, and tied to the original task context.

Agent Cards in the Enterprise

In an enterprise context, Agent Cards are transformative. Sales agents can automatically discover finance agents when margin analysis is needed. Procurement agents can find supplier agents across partner networks. External partner agents can advertise services safely within controlled boundaries.

Salesboom's AI agents expose Agent Cards that describe CRM-centric capabilities including lead qualification, opportunity analysis, revenue forecasting, pipeline management, and customer risk detection. This makes critical CRM intelligence discoverable and usable across the entire organization without manual integration work.
