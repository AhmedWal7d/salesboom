Why Oracle's Buyout of Siebel Changes Everything

The technology landscape shifted dramatically when Oracle announced its acquisition of Siebel Systems. While mergers are common in the enterprise software world, this particular deal creates unique challenges for Siebel CRM On Demand customers.

The core issue is technical incompatibility: Siebel CRM On Demand runs exclusively on IBM's DB2 database and WebSphere platform, hosted in IBM data centers. Oracle, as a database competitor to IBM, has zero incentive to maintain and support a product built entirely on rival technology.

Industry analysts, technology journalists, and even competing CEOs agree on one thing: Siebel CRM On Demand's days are numbered. The only question is not if, but when Oracle will sunset the service, leaving current customers scrambling for alternatives.

The Situation Creates Urgent Risks

  • Service degradation as Oracle prioritizes its own technology stack
  • Lack of future innovation and feature development
  • Potential forced migration to Oracle-based platforms at inopportune times
  • Sales team disruption leading to reduced customer support quality
  • Integration headaches as Oracle focuses on PeopleSoft and J.D. Edwards acquisitions
  • Uncertainty around pricing, contracts, and long-term viability

Industry Professionals Predict Siebel CRM On Demand's Demise

Don't just take our word for it. Leading industry analysts, researchers, and technology journalists have publicly stated their concerns about the future of Siebel CRM On Demand following Oracle's acquisition.

ComputerWorld's Stark Prediction

"Oracle's move to buy Siebel guarantees one thing: Siebel CRM on Demand is toast. After all, why on earth would Oracle support a product that is designed to run on IBM's DB2 database? Trust me, it won't."

Mark Hall, Editor at Large, ComputerWorld

His colleague Don Tennant added context about Oracle's existing challenges: "Of course, Oracle is still grappling with the integration headaches stemming from its acquisition of PeopleSoft and, by extension, J.D. Edwards, so dealing with the integration of Siebel CRM On Demand at the same time is only going to make those headaches throb more."

Wall Street Analysts See Opportunity for Competitors

SG Cowen & Co. analyst Peter Goldmacher called Oracle's Siebel CRM On Demand takeover "a dream come true" for other on-demand hosted CRM software providers. In his research note, Goldmacher forecasts that the deal will lead to turmoil in Siebel's CRM On Demand sales organization and prompt a number of its top sellers to look elsewhere for work.

This sales team disruption translates directly to reduced support quality and attention for existing customers as talented professionals jump ship to more stable platforms.

Competing CEO's Bold Statement

"Siebel CRM on Demand, a joint venture between Siebel and IBM, will be the first to be buried. Siebel CRM on Demand is written exclusively on DB2 and Websphere and runs in IBM data centers. Oracle will kill it. Oracle does not sell DB2."

Marc Benioff, CEO, Salesforce.com

While Benioff clearly has competitive motivations, his technical reasoning is sound and echoed by independent analysts.

Independent Research Firms Confirm Concerns

"Oracle has much more incentive to optimize for their own software. I can certainly see this is going to be an issue going forward for DB2 customers."

Sheryl Kingstone, Analyst, Yankee Group


"It's going to be an uncomfortable relationship."

David Bradshaw, Analyst, Ovum Ltd.

With respected firms like Yankee Group raising doubts about Siebel CRM On Demand's future, current customers have every reason to be concerned about their investment and business continuity.

The Real Impact on Siebel CRM On Demand Customers

If you're currently using Siebel CRM On Demand, this acquisition creates immediate and long-term risks that demand attention. Understanding these impacts helps you make informed decisions about your CRM strategy moving forward.

Business Continuity Uncertainty

You have no clear timeline for when Oracle might discontinue the service or force migration to Oracle-based platforms. This uncertainty makes long-term business planning difficult and creates unnecessary stress for IT teams and executives.

Technology Stagnation

With Oracle focused on integrating PeopleSoft, J.D. Edwards, and its own technology stack, expect minimal innovation or feature development for Siebel CRM On Demand. Your CRM solution will fall behind competitors while your business needs continue evolving.

Support Quality Degradation

As predicted by industry analysts, Siebel's top sales professionals and support staff are already looking for opportunities elsewhere. You'll likely experience longer response times, reduced expertise, and less attentive customer service as the team fractures.

Integration Challenges

Oracle's massive integration workload from multiple acquisitions means Siebel CRM On Demand becomes a low priority. Expect delayed bug fixes, compatibility issues with other business tools, and reduced attention to customer feedback.

Forced Migration on Oracle's Timeline

When Oracle decides to sunset Siebel CRM On Demand, you'll have limited time to find alternatives, migrate data, and train staff. Making the decision now on your terms is far better than being forced to react in a crisis.

Uncertain Costs and Contract Terms

Oracle could change pricing, increase fees, or modify contract terms unfavorably. Moving to a stable, independent provider now protects you from future price shocks and gives you predictable costs.
