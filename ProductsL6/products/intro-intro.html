Understanding Customer Relationship Management: CRM in Brief

CRM stands for Customer Relationship Management, though some prefer to call it Client Relationship Management.

At its core, CRM software allows businesses to manage business relationships along with the data and information associated with them.

Built Around People

Successful CRM software solutions are built around people and relationships. In any business, you need to establish strong connections with your customers.

As a business owner, you connect with the people who need your products or services, and CRM helps you nurture those connections at scale.

Strategy and Technology

CRM is both a strategy and technology used to build stronger relationships between organizations and their clients.

Organizations store information related to their clients, and employees analyze it for forecasting and reporting. Some CRM strategies include planning targeted marketing campaigns toward specific clients.

Important to Realize: CRM is more than just softwareit's a customer-centric philosophy that must infiltrate an entire organization.

Why CRM Has Become Business-Critical Software

There are many reasons why CRM software has become the most important software in the last decade.

Competition has become highly intense in the global market, and it has become easier for clients to switch companies if they are not happy with the service they receive or want products at lower prices.

One of the primary goals of CRM solutions is to retain clients and protect them from competitor temptations. When CRM is used effectively, an organization can build relationships with their clients that last a lifetime.

Personalized Experiences

Customers expect personalized experiences across all touchpoints. CRM enables businesses to deliver tailored interactions at every stage of the customer journey.

Competitive Advantage

Competition for customer attention is fiercer than ever. Data-driven decisions powered by CRM are now essential for competitive advantage in any market.

Cost Efficiency

Retaining existing customers is 5-25 times more cost-effective than acquiring new ones. CRM focuses on customer retention and lifetime value maximization.

Why Growing Businesses Need More Than Spreadsheets

Today, growing businesses manage customer connections and information in a variety of waysbut traditional methods create significant problems as you grow.

Legacy Methods

Some use old-fashioned note cards and Rolodex systems. Others store information on mobile phones. Others use Excel spreadsheets or Google documents.

Scaling Problems

Information gets lost. Team members can't access critical data when needed. No single source of truth. Tracking across touchpoints becomes impossible.

Cloud Solution

A Cloud CRM System collects your precious business data in one place, makes it accessible via the cloud, and frees your time to focus on delighting customers.

People, Process, and Technology: The CRM Foundation

Once thought of as simply a type of software, CRM has evolved into a customer-centric philosophy that must infiltrate an entire organization.

People

The people throughout a company, from the CEO to each customer service representative, need to buy in to and support CRM. Everyone must understand how CRM benefits their specific role.

Process

A company's business processes must be reengineered to strengthen its CRM initiative from the perspective of "How can this process better serve the customer?"

Technology

CRM software and technology must tie together people and processes to provide a comprehensive picture of the customer, enabling better relationships and decisions.

360-Degree Customer View: All Information in One Place

Just think of Cloud-Based CRM as a tool where, when you open a customer's account, you can see all the consolidated data about that account.

Complete Customer Information

See contact information, account history, sales, cases, invoices, quotes, files, emails, faxes, letters, notes, and any other pertinent informationall readily available online, anywhere the Internet is available.

Critical Questions Every Business Should Ask

To determine whether your business is ready for CRM or how CRM can transform your operations, consider these essential questions.

Customer Understanding

  • What are your customer satisfaction levels?
  • What is your customer attrition rate?
  • What are your customers' needs?
  • Do you focus new products around customer feedback?

Team Collaboration

  • Do Sales, Service, and Support work together?
  • Do you build processes around servicing customers better?
  • Is customer information accessible to everyone who needs it?

Data Management

  • Do you consolidate all customer data into one repository?
  • Do you integrate key systems to interact with each other?
  • Can you generate comprehensive reports on customer behavior?
  • Is your customer data secure and compliant?
The Solution: All these needs are regular requirements of any thriving and successful business. The solution that helps companies address such needs with the click of a button is what we refer to as Cloud CRM.
